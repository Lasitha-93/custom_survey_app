Floyd Mayweather defeated Saul "Canelo" Alvarez by majority decision in their super welterweight title fight Saturday in Las Vegas, but the victory was overshadowed by scoring controversy. Two judges favored Mayweather (117-111, 116-112), while judge C.J. Ross scored it a 114-114 draw, drawing boos from the crowd and criticism from boxing analysts. Mayweather (45-0) dominated Alvarez with superior defense and accuracy, landing 46% of his punches to Alvarez's 22%. The 23-year-old Mexican fighter (42-1-1) acknowledged frustration, stating, "I couldn't catch him... he's very elusive." Mayweather called Ross's scorecard "a joke," referencing her involvement in the controversial 2012 Manny Pacquiao-Timothy Bradley decision. The bout set financial records, with Mayweather guaranteed $41.5 million—the highest purse in boxing history. The event generated over $20 million at the gate and aimed to break pay-per-view records. Despite the loss, Alvarez vowed to rebound, saying, "I'm 23... I'm still going to work to fight big fights." Post-fight analysis widely condemned Ross's scoring, with trainer Teddy Atlas calling it "another black eye for boxing."
