LOS ANGELES — Fans determined the winners at Wednesday’s 39th People’s Choice Awards, setting a record with over 475 million online votes. Hosted by "The Big Bang Theory" star Kaley Cuoco, the ceremony honored audience favorites across film, television, and music. "The Hunger Games" dominated with four awards, including favorite movie and action movie, while star Jennifer Lawrence won favorite movie actress and face of heroism. Chris Colfer ("Glee"), named favorite comedic TV actor, acknowledged fan influence: "These are the people who keep you employed." Sandra Bullock received recognition for her post-Hurricane Katrina charity work at Warren Easton High School. Performances by Christina Aguilera, Jason Aldean, and Alicia Keys highlighted the CBS broadcast. Aldean noted the significance of representing country music, endorsing favorite TV crime drama winner "Castle." Attendees included Jennifer Aniston, Johnny Galecki, Matt Bomer, and Taylor Swift. Producer Chuck Lorre confirmed Angus T. Jones will return to "Two and a Half Men" next week following his public apology about the show. Unlike traditional awards, all categories use "favorite" instead of "best" and include unconventional honors like "Favorite Movie Superhero."
