As the Masters approaches, defending champion Jordan Spieth headlines the favorites despite inconsistent form this season. The 22-year-old American, who won by four strokes last year, has finished first or second in both his Augusta appearances. Bubba Watson, a two-time Masters winner (2012, 2014), thrives on the course’s layout with his power and shot-shaping, entering strong after a win at Riviera and a runner-up at Doral. Rory McIlroy, seeking a career Grand Slam, aims to overcome past Augusta struggles after closing with 68-66 in 2015. Phil Mickelson, a three-time champion, leverages course knowledge and a refined swing under new coach Andrew Getson, fresh off a Pebble Beach runner-up. Adam Scott, the 2013 winner, reunites with caddie Steve Williams following consecutive wins at the Honda Classic and WGC-Cadillac Championship. World No. 1 Jason Day rebounded from early-season ailments with victories at the Arnold Palmer Invitational and WGC-Match Play. Top contenders include Rickie Fowler, who won in Dubai but seeks major consistency; Justin Rose, last year’s joint runner-up; and Dustin Johnson, a power hitter with 10 major top-10s. Hideki Matsuyama, fifth in 2015, won Phoenix this year, while Louis Oosthuizen returns after a 2012 playoff loss. Charl Schwartzel, the 2011 champion, ended a PGA Tour drought by winning the Valspar Championship.
