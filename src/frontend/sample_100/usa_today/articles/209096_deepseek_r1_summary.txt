**Hurricane Katrina's Devastation and Legacy: A Timeline** Hurricane Katrina struck the U.S. Gulf Coast on August 29, 2005, causing catastrophic flooding in New Orleans after levee failures and killing at least 1,833 people. The storm exposed critical failures in emergency response and initiated a prolonged recovery effort. **Initial Impact and Chaos** Katrina made landfall as a Category 3 hurricane after intensifying to Category 5. Mandatory evacuations were ordered, with 25,000 residents sheltering in New Orleans' Superdome. Levees breached in multiple locations, flooding 80% of the city under up to 20 feet of water. Thousands were stranded on rooftops, while looting and violence hampered rescues. Evacuations from the Superdome and convention center faced severe delays, with officials pleading for federal aid. **Critical Failures and Early Response** FEMA's response was widely criticized, culminating in Director Michael Brown's resignation on September 12. Congress approved $10.5 billion in initial aid on September 2, followed by $51.8 billion days later. Tragedies included police shootings of six unarmed civilians on the Danziger Bridge on September 4, resulting in two deaths and later federal convictions for officers involved. Hurricane Rita struck on September 24, displacing 1.8 million people. **Long-Term Recovery Challenges** By late 2005, the death toll exceeded 1,300, with 1.3 million displaced across all 50 states. Insurance disputes stalled rebuilding, leading to lawsuits against providers. Levee repairs took months, with full drainage requiring 80 days. Recovery lagged: By 2007, only 66% of New Orleans' population had returned, and critical infrastructure remained unrepaired. FEMA trailers were found to have hazardous formaldehyde levels in 2008, forcing evacuations. **Legacy and Accountability** By Katrina's 10th anniversary in 2015, rebuilding continued with $10 billion allocated, though $2.5 billion remained unused. Former Mayor Ray Nagin was convicted of corruption in 2014 and sentenced to 10 years. Five police officers received prison terms for the Danziger Bridge killings. The storm's total damage reached $125 billion, underscoring enduring vulnerabilities in disaster preparedness and equity.
