Former NBA player Greg Oden was arrested Friday in Lawrence, Indiana, on preliminary battery charges after allegedly assaulting his ex-girlfriend. Police responded around 3:30 a.m. to a domestic disturbance at his mother's Maple Bluff Place residence, where they found the 24-year-old victim with facial injuries including swelling, lacerations, and bleeding. According to the Lawrence Police report, a witness—identified as the victim's best friend—stated Oden punched the woman in the face. Officers observed blood on furniture and a knocked-over flower pot at the scene. The victim was initially uncooperative but was urged by her friend to disclose the assault. Oden, 26, reportedly admitted fault to officers, stating, "I was wrong, and I know what has to happen." Police described him as calm and cooperative during the encounter. The altercation occurred during an argument between Oden and his ex-girlfriend, whom he dated for two years before their breakup two months ago. A relative at the home noted such arguments frequently followed their outings. Oden was transported to Marion County Jail. Prosecutors will seek a 72-hour continuance at his initial hearing to determine formal charges while a bond amount is set. The 2007 No. 1 NBA draft pick's career was derailed by persistent knee injuries, limiting him to 82 games over five seasons before a brief stint with Miami in 2013-14.
