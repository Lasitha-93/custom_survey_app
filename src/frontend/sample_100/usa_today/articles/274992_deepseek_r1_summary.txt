Michael Phelps defeated rival Ryan Lochte by more than a second in the men's 100-meter butterfly at the Bulldog Grand Slam in Athens, Ga., on Friday. Phelps clocked 51.67 seconds, the third-fastest time globally this year and breaking the 52-second barrier he'd targeted. This marks Phelps' fourth meet since ending his retirement. His time was less than half a second off his London Olympic gold-medal performance (51.21), signaling strong progress with the Rio Games two years away. Phelps expressed satisfaction but noted room for improvement on his turn and finish. Lochte, competing for the first time in over two months, acknowledged Phelps swam "a fantastic race." Lochte is recovering from a re-injured left knee, originally hurt in a November fan interaction. Despite the setback, he posted solid times in the 100 fly and 200 freestyle. Both swimmers are using this meet as preparation for the U.S. National Championships starting August 6th in Irvine, Calif., where they are expected to face off again in the 100 fly. Results from Nationals and the Pan Pacific Championships will determine the U.S. team for the 2015 World Championships. Phelps has won Olympic gold in the 100 fly at the past three Games.
