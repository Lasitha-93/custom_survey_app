**Photographer Jason Bell Shares Top Tips After Royal Christening Assignment** Award-winning portrait photographer Jason Bell, known for capturing Prince George's first official photos and celebrities like David Beckham and Halle Berry, reveals his essential photography advice. Despite no cameras at the royal christening, Bell was the sole invited photographer. His tips, drawn from decades of experience, emphasize authenticity and professionalism: 1. **Start Young**: Bell began taking pictures at age five. Access to a school darkroom at 14 solidified his passion. 2. **Do Homework**: Research subjects thoroughly to reveal hidden depths. Being a "people person" is crucial for drawing out genuine moments. 3. **Get Educated**: Though he studied philosophy, politics, and economics, editing his university newspaper taught him to photograph celebrities without intimidation. 4. **Focus**: Specialize early and practice relentlessly to develop a distinct style. Avoid diversifying prematurely. 5. **Learn Film Shooting**: Film’s unforgiving nature instills discipline. Over-reliance on digital post-production is "lazy." 6. **Stay Calm**: Handle high-pressure situations—like directing 12 movie stars—with composure. "Don’t freak out" is non-negotiable. 7. **Highlight Best Traits**: Enhance subjects’ natural features without altering their essence. Respect their desire to look authentic, not just "beautiful." 8. **Say No**: Select assignments strategically to protect your brand. An agent helps avoid overcommitting. 9. **Engage Subjects**: Use humor ("say cheese" ironically) to relax people. The camera grants authority to shed personal shyness. 10. **Never Question Selection**: When chosen for prestigious jobs like the royal christening, project confidence instead of asking "Why me?" Bell, pleased with the public reaction to the christening photos, calls the experience "moving" and would gladly repeat it. His approach blends technical rigor with deep human connection.
