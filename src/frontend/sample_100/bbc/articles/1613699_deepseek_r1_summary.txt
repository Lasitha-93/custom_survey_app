Commons Speaker John Bercow has formally requested Buckingham Palace grant permission for the body of veteran Labour politician Tony Benn to rest overnight in the Chapel of St Mary Undercroft within the Palace of Westminster before his funeral. Mr Bercow cited Benn's exceptional parliamentary service spanning over 50 years as an MP and his rare award of the Freedom of the House as justification for the honour. If approved by the Palace, Benn's body would rest privately in the chapel on March 26th, the eve of his funeral service at St Margaret's Church opposite Parliament. A parliamentary source confirmed this would be a private occasion solely for family, stressing it is distinct from a public lying-in-state. The chapel, sometimes used for weddings and christenings by parliamentary families, would host a vigil kept overnight by the Speaker's chaplain, Rev Rose Hudson-Wilkin. Approval would make Benn only the second parliamentarian accorded this privilege, following Baroness Thatcher whose coffin rested there overnight before her ceremonial funeral last April. Unlike Thatcher's, Benn's funeral will not be ceremonial. Tony Benn, a major figure on the Labour left, former cabinet minister, anti-war campaigner, and prolific diarist, died at home aged 88 on March 14th. MPs will pay tribute to him in a special Commons debate led by Deputy Prime Minister Nick Clegg and Labour's Harriet Harman on Thursday.
