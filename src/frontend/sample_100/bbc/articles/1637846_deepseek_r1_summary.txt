Eight French nationals were among ten people killed when a Greek F-16 fighter jet crashed during take-off at Los Llanos air base in Albacete, Spain, on Monday. Spain's defence ministry stated the jet "lost power" around 15:30 local time, crashing 260km southeast of Madrid. Both Greek pilots died, with local media reporting one performed an incorrect manoeuvre. At least five other aircraft were damaged in the incident. Reports indicate many of the injured are French or Italian. Personnel from the US, Denmark, Germany, Belgium, and the UK were also stationed at the base, participating in NATO's Tactical Leadership Programme (TLP) training exercise. The TLP, involving 10 NATO nations, aims to enhance allied air force leadership skills but operates outside NATO's formal structure. Emergency crews extinguished the resulting fire and assessed damage. Aviation analyst Sean Maffett noted the crash occurred unusually far from the runway. NATO Secretary General Jens Stoltenberg expressed "deep sadness," calling it "a tragedy which affects the whole NATO family." French Defence Minister Jean-Yves Le Drian will visit the crash site on Tuesday.
