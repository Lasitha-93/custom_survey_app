Facebook will launch a satellite in 2016 to provide internet access to remote parts of Africa, founder Mark Zuckerberg announced. The project is a partnership with French satellite provider Eutelsat. The initiative is part of Facebook's Internet.org effort, which aims to connect underserved populations globally but has faced criticism, particularly in India, over concerns it gives Facebook an unfair market advantage. Zuckerberg stated the satellite leverages existing technology to offer affordable access where traditional infrastructure is impractical. Users will be able to connect using standard, affordable equipment. Eutelsat confirmed the service, optimised for community access, will launch in the second half of 2016, targeting users beyond terrestrial network coverage. This satellite approach joins Facebook's exploration of drones and aircraft to beam internet connectivity. While satellite internet exists, its high cost has previously limited accessibility in developing regions.
