Liberia's Education Minister Etmonia David-Tarpeh expressed disbelief after all 25,000 candidates failed this year's University of Liberia admission exam. The unprecedented failure rate means no new first-year students will enroll when the state-run university reopens next month. University spokesman Momodu Getaweh attributed the results to students lacking basic English skills and enthusiasm, stating: "In English, the mechanics of the language, they didn't know anything about it." Candidates paid $25 each to take the test. Minister David-Tarpeh told BBC Focus on Africa she would meet university officials to review the results, calling the outcome "like mass murder" and questioning its validity. Students described shattered dreams. The failure highlights Liberia's struggling education system, which President Ellen Johnson Sirleaf recently acknowledged remains "in a mess" despite the country's recovery from a civil war that ended a decade ago. Schools face shortages of qualified teachers and basic materials. The university maintains its decision stands and urged moving beyond "emotion," with Getaweh emphasizing: "The war has ended 10 years ago now. We have to put that behind us."
