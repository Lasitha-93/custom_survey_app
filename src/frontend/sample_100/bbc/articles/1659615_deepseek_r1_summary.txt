Bollywood star Anil Kapoor will headline the Indian remake of the acclaimed US counter-terrorism series "24," producers announced. The 53-year-old actor, known internationally for "Slumdog Millionaire," will portray the lead role mirroring Kiefer Sutherland’s iconic character Jack Bauer. Kapoor, who has appeared in over 100 Bollywood films and featured in the American "24" as Middle Eastern leader Omar Hassan, is also producing the Mumbai-set adaptation through his company Anil Kapoor Film. "I am not playing it safe, I am playing the lead. I'm a movie star," Kapoor told Reuters, describing the project as a significant risk. Director Abhinay Deo expressed confidence, stating the remake combines Kapoor’s star power with locally resonant subject matter. The production replicates the original series’ counter-terrorism unit set. Colors channel CEO Raj Nayak noted the Hindi-language version targets audiences unfamiliar with the original, calling it a "novelty" for India’s heartland. The series, adapting the 2001-2010 show that spanned 192 episodes, is slated to air later this year.
