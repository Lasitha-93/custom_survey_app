Two women have pleaded not guilty to murdering a two-year-old boy and blaming his death on a seven-year-old child. Rachael Fee, 31, and her civil partner Nyomi Fee, 28, denied murdering Liam Johnson (also known as Liam Fee) by inflicting blunt force trauma to his head and body at their Thornton, Fife home between March 15 and 22, 2014. They also pleaded not guilty to attempting to pervert the course of justice by falsely attributing Liam's death to another child when speaking to emergency services and others. Additionally, both face charges of neglecting Liam over a two-year period ending in March 2014, including allegations they confined him in a pushchair for prolonged periods, covered his face with a blanket, taped socks to his hands, deprived him of adequate food and stimulation, and administered inappropriate medication to sedate him. The pair further denied charges of ill-treating two other unnamed boys during the same period. Prosecutors allege they forced one boy to stand naked under cold showers as punishment for bed-wetting, locked him in a makeshift cage constructed from a fire guard and wood, and tied him naked to a chair in a room containing snakes while threatening that a boa constrictor would "eat naughty boys." A trial has been scheduled for April 4 next year at Livingston High Court, with prosecutors estimating proceedings could last eight weeks. Both accused currently reside in Ryton, Tyne and Wear.
