Twitter shares plunged to record lows after reporting slower-than-expected user growth for Q1 2014. The stock fell over 11% in after-hours trading, dipping below its $38.80 IPO price. The social network reported 255 million active users, a 5.8% quarterly increase. While this reversed four consecutive quarters of declining growth and exceeded the previous quarter's 3.8% rise, it still fell short of analyst forecasts. Twitter posted a net loss of $132 million for the quarter. Revenue reached $250 million, with $226 million derived from advertising. CEO Dick Costolo emphasized the revenue growth, attributing it to "increased engagement and user growth." He noted the integration of MoPub advertising software helped reach over 1 billion iOS and Android users monthly. Timeline views, another engagement metric, rose 15% to 157 billion. Despite these gains, Twitter's user base remains significantly smaller than Facebook's 1.28 billion active users. WhatsApp, recently acquired by Facebook, also surpassed 500 million users.
