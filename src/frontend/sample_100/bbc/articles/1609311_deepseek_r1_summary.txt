Northern Ireland's Social Development Minister Mervyn Storey faces a 17:00 GMT deadline today to submit a report on a special adviser's conduct to a Stormont committee. The Assembly's Social Development Committee is investigating allegations of political interference within the Housing Executive, stemming from a 2013 BBC Spotlight programme. The programme alleged DUP special adviser Stephen Brimstone pressured DUP councillor Jenny Palmer to change her vote during a Housing Executive board meeting. Officials conducted an internal review of Mr. Brimstone's conduct, but it remains unpublished. Committee Chair Alex Maskey previously warned that failure to produce the report by February 12th would lead the committee to court. Last week, the committee met privately and extended the deadline for Minister Storey to February 18th. A committee spokesperson stated that, subject to the minister's response, all options would be considered to resolve the matter satisfactorily.
