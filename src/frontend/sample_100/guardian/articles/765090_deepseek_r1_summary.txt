Gary Neville condemned refereeing as "scandalous" after Chelsea's youth team controversially defeated Valencia 5-4 in a UEFA Youth League penalty shoot-out. The quarter-final match ended 1-1 at Chelsea's Cobham training ground before spot-kicks decided the outcome. Valencia's first penalty, taken by Alberto Gil, was disallowed when officials ruled it struck the post. However, video footage showed the ball crossed the goal line after hitting the stanchion and bouncing clear. Players protested extensively, and Neville tweeted his disbelief. His brother Phil Neville shared an image confirming the ball entered the net. Valencia first-team players Antonio Barragán and Paco Alcácer also criticized the decision on social media. The club announced it will formally appeal to UEFA, stating: "We trust UEFA will give an appropriate response." Valencia acknowledged Chelsea's sportsmanship in recognizing the error.
