As Magical Mystery Tour receives a reissue, the Beatles' multifaceted cinematic legacy comes into focus. Despite initial reluctance, their documentary *Let It Be* earned the band their sole Academy Award in 1971 for Best Original Song Score, preserving a fractious recording period they had sought to bury. Post-breakup, Apple Films—originally financing *Magical Mystery Tour*—enabled diverse projects: John Lennon’s avant-garde collaborations with Yoko Ono, Ringo Starr’s direction of a T. Rex concert film, and George Harrison’s production of documentaries and the rediscovered dark comedy *Little Malcolm and His Struggle Against the Eunuchs*, starring John Hurt. As actors, the Beatles debuted in 1964’s *A Hard Day’s Night*, followed by *Help!* and *Magical Mystery Tour*. Lennon later starred in *How I Won the War*, while Ringo sustained an acting career with roles opposite Peter Sellers and Frank Zappa. Their music rarely features directly in films due to stringent approval requirements, leading to cover-heavy adaptations like the critically panned *Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band*. Impersonations range from animated series to comedies like *Walk Hard*, reflecting enduring cultural fascination.
