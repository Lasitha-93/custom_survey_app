**Portuguese Bestseller Urges Euro Exit Amid Economic Crisis** A book advocating Portugal's immediate exit from the eurozone has become an unlikely national bestseller, reflecting deep public frustration with austerity measures. Economist João Ferreira do Amaral's "Porque devemos sair do Euro" (Why We Must Leave the Euro) argues abandoning the euro is the only path to restoring competitiveness. Now in its fourth printing since April, the book frequently ranks among Portugal's top 10 sellers. Its popularity underscores widespread discontent as Portugal faces a third consecutive year of negative growth in 2013, stubbornly high unemployment (17.5%), and slow deficit reduction despite severe government austerity. Ferreira do Amaral, a longstanding Eurosceptic, insists his call for a return to the escudo is driven by economic necessity, not nostalgia. He contends the euro's strength cripples Portuguese industry and that current policies are failing. "It is clear to people... they want to look at all the other alternatives," he stated. While the Portuguese government firmly rejects any possibility of a "Pexit," Ferreira do Amaral has become a prominent figure in televised debates, declaring: "Now a taboo is broken." The book's success signals a significant shift in public discourse amid prolonged economic hardship. *(Source: Guardian Weekly, incorporating material from Le Monde)*
