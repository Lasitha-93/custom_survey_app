More than 650 dentists in England and Wales earned over £250,000 last year, NHS statistics reveal. Of 19,500 practising dentists, 654 exceeded this threshold—including 382 earning above £300,000. This equates to one in 30 dentists. The average income for all dentists was £89,062. Those running NHS-contracted practices reported average taxable earnings of £345,651, while dentists without NHS contracts averaged £65,697, rising to £99,208 after expenses. The data covers self-employed dentists performing NHS work during 2007-08, under a contract introduced in 2006 to replace "drill and fill" payments with activity-based units. The disclosure may renew criticism of dental services, following reports of inadequate public access. Health Secretary Andy Burnham pledged reforms in June after an independent inquiry. NHS Information Centre chief Tim Straughan noted earnings "vary greatly" based on contract status. Health Minister Ann Keen stated NHS dentistry is improving, citing record investment and expanded services.
