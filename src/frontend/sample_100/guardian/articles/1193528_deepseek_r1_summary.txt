The Channel 4 sitcom "Catastrophe," created by and starring Sharon Horgan and Rob Delaney, follows an American man and an Irish woman whose brief London fling results in an unplanned pregnancy. After a six-day encounter, Rob (Delaney) returns to the U.S., only for Sharon (Horgan) to inform him she's expecting. He immediately relocates to London and proposes, subverting traditional sitcom tropes by beginning where most rom-coms end. Inspired by the writers' own Twitter-initiated friendship, the series explores the chaotic realities of impending parenthood between near-strangers. It blends sharp humor with serious themes, including advanced maternal age risks (Sharon faces Down's syndrome concerns and is labeled a "geriatric mum") and a cervical dysplasia scare. Their cultural clashes intensify, notably during arguments over religious christening versus secular ceremonies. The show features Carrie Fisher in a standout role as Rob's acerbic mother, Mia, who dismisses the pregnancy as "just a foreign baby." Despite tackling heavy subjects, "Catastrophe" maintains its comedic edge through brutally honest dialogue and visceral scenarios, such as Rob proposing in a urine puddle and graphic childbirth warnings. The second series continues to chart the couple's turbulent journey through parenthood and partnership.
