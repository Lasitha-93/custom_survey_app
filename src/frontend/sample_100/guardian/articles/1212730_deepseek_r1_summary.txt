Virginia state senator Creigh Deeds was released from the hospital Friday, four days after being stabbed by his son during an altercation at their Millboro home. Deeds expressed gratitude to supporters via social media, stating, "I am alive so must live. Some wounds won't heal." The incident occurred Tuesday. Deeds, a former Democratic gubernatorial candidate, suffered multiple stab wounds to the head and chest during a confrontation with his 24-year-old son, Gus Deeds. Gus Deeds died at the scene from an apparent self-inflicted gunshot wound. Police recovered a gun but haven't detailed the stabbing weapon. Officials revealed Gus Deeds had undergone a psychiatric evaluation the day before the attack but was released because no hospital bed was available. This failure to admit him has prompted an investigation by Virginia's director of inspector general for behavioral health. Senator Deeds was airlifted to the University of Virginia Medical Center following the attack and discharged Friday morning. Gus Deeds had studied music at the College of William and Mary, withdrawing last month despite a strong academic record. Creigh Deeds ran unsuccessfully for Virginia attorney general in 2005 and for governor in 2009.
