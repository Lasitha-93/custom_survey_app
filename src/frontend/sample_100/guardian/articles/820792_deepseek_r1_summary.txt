Belarusian President Alexander Lukashenko has pardoned six jailed opposition figures, including prominent 2010 presidential candidate Nikolai Statkevich, his administration announced Saturday. Statkevich, 59, was the last of roughly 10 politicians imprisoned after challenging Lukashenko in the 2010 election, widely condemned by the West as fraudulent. Lukashenko, in power since 1994 and seeking a fifth term this October, cited "humane principles" for the move. The releases drew immediate international approval. The European Union called it "important progress" toward better relations, while the U.S. State Department termed it "an important step toward normalizing relations." Both urged Belarus to fully restore the political rights of the freed individuals and other former political prisoners. Lukashenko, long ostracized by Western governments over his suppression of dissent, has shown tentative signs of thawing ties this year by distancing Belarus from Russia's Ukraine policies and reviving contacts with the EU and U.S. The EU, a key trade partner, stressed that further progress hinges on democratic reforms. Top EU officials Federica Mogherini and Johannes Hahn demanded Belarus lift "all restrictions" on the released activists' civil and political rights. Statkevich had served six years since 2011 for organizing post-election protests. Analysts suggest the pardons aim to improve Lukashenko's international standing ahead of October's vote, with Minsk-based expert Alexander Klaskovsky noting Western approval was unlikely while political prisoners remained jailed.
