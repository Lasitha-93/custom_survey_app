A Turkish court has charged three Vice News journalists with "aiding a terrorist organisation" and ordered them detained pending trial, sparking international condemnation. British correspondent Jake Hanrahan, cameraman Philip Pendlebury, and their Turkey-based fixer were arrested last Thursday in Diyarbakir while covering clashes between security forces and the Patriotic Revolutionary Youth Movement, linked to the outlawed Kurdistan Workers' Party (PKK). Their lawyer, Ahmet Ay, called the accusations "entirely baseless," revealing police acted on an anonymous tip falsely claiming the journalists supported Islamic State. Interrogation questions bore no relation to ISIS, he noted. A driver arrested with them has been released. Vice News' European head Kevin Sutcliffe denounced the charges as intimidation tactics to censor coverage of southeastern Turkey's unrest. Amnesty International, PEN International, and the Committee to Protect Journalists demanded immediate release, warning Turkey routinely misuses anti-terror laws to suppress dissent. Amnesty researcher Andrew Gardner stated the arrests reflect a government effort to muzzle reporting on escalating violence in Kurdish regions, where police abuses and civilian deaths have surged. The detinations occur amid renewed conflict between Ankara and the PKK, following the collapse of a 2012 ceasefire. Turkey has launched airstrikes, arrested hundreds on terrorism charges, and shuttered Kurdish media outlets. Turkey holds the notorious distinction as the world's leading jailer of journalists. No trial date has been set.
