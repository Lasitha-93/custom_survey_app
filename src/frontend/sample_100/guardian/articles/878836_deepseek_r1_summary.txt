Germany delivered a stunning 4-0 victory over Australia in their World Cup opener, showcasing their youthful squad's dominance. Lukas Podolski opened the scoring early (8'), followed by Miroslav Klose's header (26'). Australia's hopes faded when Tim Cahill received a controversial straight red card (59'), and Thomas Mueller (68') plus substitute Cacau (70') sealed the emphatic win. In other Group D action, Ghana defeated Serbia 1-0. Serbia's Aleksandar Lukovic was sent off (74'), and Asamoah Gyan converted a decisive penalty (84') after Zdravko Kuzmanovic handled the ball. Slovenia claimed a 1-0 Group C win against Algeria after Algerian substitute Abdelkader Ghezzal was dismissed (73'). Robert Koren's long-range strike (79') was fumbled by goalkeeper Faouzi Chaouchi for the winner.
