Comedian Frankie Boyle has reignited controversy by repeating offensive jokes about Olympic swimmer Rebecca Adlington's appearance during the London Games. Boyle tweeted to his 800,000 followers last week that Adlington might have "an unfair advantage in the swimming by possessing a dolphin's face," echoing derogatory remarks he made during her 2008 Beijing gold medal wins. This mirrors Boyle's 2008 comments on BBC's Mock the Week, where he described Adlington as looking "pretty weird" and implied she was "very dirty." The BBC Trust later ruled those jokes "humiliating" and "offensive," stating they should never have aired. Despite 75 initial complaints and the Trust's censure, Boyle refused to apologize and subsequently left the show, though he denies being fired. Comedian David Walliams publicly defended Adlington on Twitter, calling her "a beautiful woman, inside and out." Adlington, unfazed, secured bronze in the 400m freestyle, swimming faster than her Beijing gold-winning time.
