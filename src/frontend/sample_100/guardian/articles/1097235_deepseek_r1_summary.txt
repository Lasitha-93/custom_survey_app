Five key Netherlands players missed full training Tuesday ahead of their World Cup last-16 clash with Mexico. Star winger Arjen Robben, defenders Stefan de Vrij and Daley Blind, and midfielder Nigel de Jong were restricted to running exercises on the sidelines at Flamengo's training ground in Rio de Janeiro. Defender Bruno Martins Indi, recovering from a concussion sustained against Australia, trained separately, practicing shooting with goalkeeper Jasper Cillessen. Substitute midfielder Leroy Fer also sat out due to a hamstring strain suffered after scoring against Chile. The absences occurred while the rest of the squad played a practice match.
