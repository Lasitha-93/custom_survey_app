Major children's games from brands including Disney, Nickelodeon, and Hasbro feature in-app purchases (IAPs) reaching £69.99, intensifying debate over freemium models targeting young audiences. A review identified 10 such titles, all rated for children as young as 4+ and available on iOS, with seven also on Android. The games and their highest-priced virtual items include: SpongeBob Moves In (£69.99 for 4,000 Jellyfish Jelly), My Little Pony – Friendship is Magic (£69.99 for 150,000 coins), Temple Run: Oz (£69.99 for 500,000 coins), Skylanders Lost Islands (£69.99 for 7,500 gems), Smurfs' Village (£69.99 for 2,000 SmurfBerries), Madagascar: Join the Circus! (£69.99 for 1,200 bananas), Snoopy's Street Fair (£69.99 for a trunk of currency), Fashion Inc. by Stardoll (£69.99 for 1,000 diamonds), Hello Kitty Kawaii Town (£69.99 for 2,000 tickets), and Gnome Village by Disney (£69.99 for 1,200 gold bars). The pricing tiers raise ethical concerns about accessibility for children, prompting questions over whether developers should cap IAPs or if parents bear responsibility for restricting purchase settings. The discussion is complicated by brands like Peanuts and Hello Kitty, which attract adult fans, blurring audience targeting. Readers are invited to debate these issues in comments.
