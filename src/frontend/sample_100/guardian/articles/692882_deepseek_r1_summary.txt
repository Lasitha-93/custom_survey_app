The UK's national parks are enhancing sustainable travel with new cycling and transport initiatives. In the Lake District, a £6.9m programme includes improved cycle paths, e-bike hires (£15-£25/day), and a dedicated bike bus from Windermere station to Whinlatter Forest or Thirlmere (daily July-August; bike fare: single ticket + £1.50). The Peak District offers the 8.5-mile Monsal Trail and Ride and Ramble walks linked to buses, with discounts on attractions like Haddon Hall. Loch Lomond visitors can cycle the West Loch Lomond path to Luss, then take water buses to Balmaha for onward routes. Exmoor's e-bikes (available at six locations) feature 20-mile battery ranges and charging points at pubs, supported by the Moor Rover minibus (£6/adult). Yorkshire Dales provides the Malham Tarn shuttle from Skipton and week-long parking tickets (£14). Brecon Beacons' Sunday buses include the Geopark Circular tour to waterfalls and distilleries, while Drover Holidays offers e-bike tours from £215pp. Snowdonia promotes heritage railways and Sherpa buses for cyclists accessing Snowdon routes. Details: nationalparks.gov.uk. (199 words)
