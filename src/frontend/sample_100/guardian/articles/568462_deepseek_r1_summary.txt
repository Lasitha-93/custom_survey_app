Chinese photographer Zhou Zhefeng has won the overall title in BBC Wildlife Magazine's photography competition with his image "Leopard path". The winning shot, captured in China, features a leopard walking along a forest path. In the Animal Portraits category, Zhou's leopard image also took the top prize. Runners-up in this category were Beatriz Estanque's "Hare in a storm" from Portugal and Javier Rivas' "Horned guan" from Guatemala. Anil Cherukupalli's "Spitting sloth bear", taken in India, was named runner-up in the Animal Behaviour category. Additionally, the magazine's editor selected two images for special recognition: Jess Isden's "Spotted bowerbird display" and Franklin Castaneda's "Mating raccoons". The winning and commended photographs will be featured in the December issue of BBC Wildlife Magazine.
