Jonny Wilkinson risks ending his England career by signing a new contract with Toulon extending his stay in France until at least 2012. The fly-half is close to agreeing a one-year deal with an additional one-year option, defying the Rugby Football Union's policy requiring elite players to be based in England after next year's World Cup to prepare for the 2015 tournament on home soil. Toulon director Philippe Saint-André confirmed the deal is "nearly done," stating Wilkinson is "very happy" in France. The 31-year-old admitted he wants to stay put, citing rejuvenation and enjoyment: "It fits the bill for me... I'm learning a hell of a lot." His standout performance against London Irish underscored his current form. The move creates a dilemma for England. RFU elite director Rob Andrew recently mandated all top players must return to the Aviva Premiership post-2021 World Cup. Wilkinson’s exceptional record (1,111 points in 80 Tests) makes him a valuable asset, but the RFU fears setting a precedent could trigger an exodus to France’s lucrative Top 14 league, which offers higher salaries. Saint-André acknowledged the RFU’s stance, predicting younger English players will stay domestically while veterans seek French opportunities. He compared the league’s popularity to football’s Premier League, noting its current priority over national team interests in France. Wilkinson suggested he’d prefer playing for England while based abroad but avoided direct conflict, stating: "There are other players in the same situation."
