Premier League chief executive Richard Scudamore faces scrutiny after private emails containing sexist language were leaked to the Sunday Mirror. The messages, automatically forwarded to a temporary secretary for diary coordination, included derogatory terms like "gash" (used by a correspondent) and references to "female irrationality." While acknowledging the emails' "pathetic" and unfunny nature, the author argues they don't constitute severe enough misogyny to render Scudamore's position untenable, defending his right to private "twattishness." However, Scudamore is condemned as a "professional hypocrite" due to his handling of a prior incident. Three years ago, Scudamore withdrew all Premier League funding from fan organization Supporters Direct after its chief executive, Dave Boyle, posted euphoric, sweary tweets following AFC Wimbledon's promotion. Boyle immediately apologized and resigned to protect the organization, but Scudamore's league still revoked funding via the Football Stadia Improvement Fund. This action jeopardized 10 jobs and lasted months. The author contends Scudamore's "petulant, spiteful" overreaction—punishing an entire organization for momentary online transgressions—reveals a more troubling character flaw than his private emails. This hypocrisy received minimal media coverage compared to the email scandal.
