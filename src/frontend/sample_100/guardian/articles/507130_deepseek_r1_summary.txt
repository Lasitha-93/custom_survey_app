Middlesex surged to the top of County Championship Division One with a dominant victory over Northamptonshire, spearheaded by Toby Roland-Jones' 12-wicket match haul. The 26-year-old seamer, boasting a career first-class average under 22, reignited discussions about a potential England call-up despite questions about his pace. In Division One's other fixture, Somerset defeated Durham to draw level with second-placed Yorkshire. Captain Marcus Trescothick's explosive 133 off just 133 balls secured a challenging fourth-innings chase of 248, fueling hopes the 38-year-old could deliver Somerset's maiden title. Division Two leaders Hampshire extended their advantage with a 278-run win against Leicestershire. All-rounder Matt Coles starred with 83 runs and three wickets, rewarding his captain's patience after inconsistent performances since joining from Kent. Worcestershire strengthened their promotion push by crushing Essex, with Saeed Ajmal claiming a career-best 13-94 and skipper Daryl Mitchell contributing 97 and 38. Kent suffered a heavy defeat to Gloucestershire, collapsing to 2-4 before Ben Harmison's century offered scant consolation. Meanwhile, Northamptonshire's David Willey signaled his return from injury with a blistering 95 off 45 balls in the NatWest T20 Blast, including six sixes, boosting his prospects for England's World Cup squad.
