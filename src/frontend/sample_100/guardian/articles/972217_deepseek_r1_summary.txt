Violent protests erupted outside Ukraine's parliament building in Kiev on Tuesday as lawmakers voted on a controversial draft law granting special status to eastern regions embroiled in conflict. One police officer was killed and several others injured when a grenade exploded during clashes between demonstrators and riot police. Protesters opposed the legislation, which addresses the status of Donbass territories held by pro-Russia separatists. Nationalist groups, including the Svoboda party, were visible among the demonstrators. Photographs showed chaotic scenes: protesters seizing police shields, officers administering emergency aid to wounded colleagues, and smoke billowing near government buildings. The legislation forms part of Ukraine's efforts to resolve the eight-year conflict in its eastern regions, though critics argue it risks legitimizing separatist forces. The clashes represent the deadliest violence in Kiev's government district since the 2014 revolution. Authorities have launched an investigation into the grenade attack amid heightened political tensions.
