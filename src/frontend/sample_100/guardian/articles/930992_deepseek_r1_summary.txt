Hong Kong police arrested 80 pro-democracy protesters and deployed pepper spray on Tuesday to clear a street blockade in Mong Kok district, the site of prolonged demonstrations against Beijing's electoral restrictions. Officers ordered dispersal via loudspeakers before firing the irritant from elevated platforms, causing injuries including eye and arm pain among demonstrators. The arrests—for contempt of court, assaulting police, and unlawful assembly—included veteran lawmaker Leung Kwok-hung and a 14-year-old boy. Elderly protester Ng Pun-tuk, 78, declared he was "prepared to go to jail" for democratic reform. This marks the second clearance since courts granted injunctions allowing authorities to dismantle protest camps that began September 28. Demonstrators oppose China's requirement that 2017 leadership candidates be vetted by a pro-Beijing committee, calling it a move to ensure a loyalist victory. Public support has waned amid transport disruptions and recent vandalism at the legislature, with an HKU poll showing 83% want blockades ended. Talks between protesters and officials collapsed last month, though Chief Secretary Carrie Lam stated negotiations remain possible if students propose a "voluntary" exit mechanism. Police are expected to clear adjacent Nathan Road on Wednesday.
