**Journalists' Gadget-Fueled Christmas Cooking Experiment Descends Into Chaos** Guardian writers Rhik Samadder and Felicity Cloake embarked on a hazardous holiday cooking challenge, misusing kitchen gadgets to create unconventional Christmas fare. The experiment, conceived by editors, nearly resulted in multiple disasters. Cloake arrived prepared, immediately crafting "sprout pops" using boiled sprouts wrapped in sausagemeat, cooked in a child's cake pop maker. Samadder countered by attempting to deep-fry brined turkey drumsticks in a digital fryer, causing dangerous oil boil-overs that nearly scalded him. A bid to create cheap "shampagne" using a SodaStream proved perilous. Samadder carbonated wine in a bathtub amid electrocution fears as the machine violently bucked, spilling fizzy wine everywhere. Guests deemed the result "bit flat" and likened it to "toilet wine." Their centerpiece – three chocolate fountains repurposed for gravy, cheese sauce, and eggnog – encountered viscosity issues. Cloake frantically adjusted consistencies while multitasking roast potatoes and pigs-in-blankets. Samadder accidentally dyed the cheese sauce nuclear orange with turmeric. The notorious "Egg Master" gadget produced its signature horror: a collapsing chocolate "Chegg Log" that slithered out "like a slug." Amidst near-disasters, Cloake salvaged the evening with a surprisingly acclaimed raw, vegan trifle featuring spiralized persimmon and cashew-banana custard. Guest verdicts were mixed: Sprout pops (4.5/5) and the trifle (5/5) triumphed. Deep-fried turkey (4/5) was edible but unwieldy. The shampagne (3/5) was palatable only due to its low cost. The Chegg Log (1/5) was merely "edible." Both journalists survived the ordeal, attributing it to "a Christmas miracle." Safety authorities strongly advise against replicating these methods.
