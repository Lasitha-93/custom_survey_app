New TV satire "Red Top," premiering on Gold January 20th, lampoons the News International phone-hacking scandal through an irreverent 1970s fantasy. The Comic Strip production, conceived by Peter Richardson, features Maxine Peake as Rebekah Brooks, depicted as a northern ingenue who accidentally becomes News International's CEO and gets embroiled in scandal. The show promises surreal plot twists, including Brooks roller-skating through a newsroom and Wendi Deng locking Rupert Murdoch in a room while spending time with Tony Blair. Set amidst flairs, moustaches, and disco music, Richardson describes it as a "Boogie Nights-style parallel universe." Preview audiences reportedly laughed aloud at the hysterical portrayals. The cast includes Harry Enfield as Ross Kemp, Stephen Mangan as Tony Blair, Russell Tovey as Andy Coulson, Nigel Planer as Rupert Murdoch, and Eleanor Matsuura as Wendi Deng, alongside cameos from Johnny Vegas and Alexei Sayle. Gold's commissioning editor Simon Lupton called the script "wonderfully hilarious and playful." "Red Top" airs at 10pm on January 20.
