The Observer Food Monthly's countdown of the 50 best cookbooks features an extract from Joël Robuchon's "The Complete Robuchon" (2008), hailed as an "Escoffier for the 21st century." The book showcases orthodox French techniques and signature dishes, including Robuchon's famed buttery mashed potatoes. Highlighted recipes include his Madeleines—touted as France's best—requiring 20 minutes preparation, an hour's refrigeration, and 12-15 minutes baking. Key tips: almond flour substitutes regular flour for nut allergies, and variations offer pistachio, lemon, chocolate, or chocolate-hazelnut twists. The Provençal beef stew, serving six, uses beef cheeks larded with bacon and pork rind to prevent drying during its four-hour cook. Marinated overnight in red wine and cognac, it improves with reheating. Lamb adaptations use white wine and lemon zest, reducing cooking to three hours. Both recipes exemplify Robuchon's precision with classic French foundations.
