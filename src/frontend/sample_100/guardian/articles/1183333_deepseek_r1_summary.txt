Stan Laurel and Oliver Hardy's 1938 comedy "Block-Heads" has been rereleased in a double bill with their classic 1932 short "The Music Box." "Block-Heads" features an absurd premise: Stan's character is accidentally left guarding a WWI trench for 20 years after the Armistice. Upon being discovered and reunited with Ollie, their chaotic domestic dynamic resumes immediately. The film escalates through a series of calamities, including Stan inadvertently mimicking a disabled veteran and causing major destruction to Ollie's car, all delivered with Hardy's signature howls of despair. Despite its wartime opening, the film maintains a tone of "pristine innocence." "The Music Box," considered one of their greatest shorts, presents a Sisyphean nightmare. Laurel and Hardy struggle to deliver a crated upright piano up a steep outdoor flight of stairs in Los Angeles, resulting in repeated, spectacular disasters. The core comedy lies in their "superbly performed grumpy stoicism" – their resigned, almost impervious reactions to utter physical and commercial ruin. The short culminates in a famously understated reaction upon learning their arduous effort was unnecessary.
