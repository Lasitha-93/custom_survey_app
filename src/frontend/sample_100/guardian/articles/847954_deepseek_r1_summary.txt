England have opened their Six Nations campaign with two victories, mirroring last year's start but with less attacking flair. Interim coach Stuart Lancaster's side sits second behind Wales after grinding wins against Scotland and Italy, relying heavily on Owen Farrell's kicking, disciplined defence, and opportunistic tries from Charlie Hodgson. This contrasts sharply with the more expansive rugby played under former manager Martin Johnson. Lancaster's overhaul included dropping established players like winger Mark Cueto, who told *The Guardian* the post-World Cup changes felt premature. Cueto defended Johnson's record, noting England had won the 2011 Six Nations and topped their World Cup pool: "To make massive changes on the back of that was tough." He believes Johnson "was the man for the job." With Wales next at Twickenham, Cueto acknowledges their favourites tag but expects a close contest. He praised England's resilience against Italy but admitted Lancaster's cautious approach—prioritizing field position over creativity—reflects a new coaching team focusing on defence first. Cueto also expressed surprise at Chris Ashton playing left wing instead of his preferred right side. England's upcoming fixtures against Wales, France, and Ireland will test whether Lancaster's rebuild can evolve into a more attacking force as he auditions for the permanent head coach role. Cueto, now running coaching clinics, remains realistic about his international future at 32 but hopes England's confidence grows from their pragmatic start.
