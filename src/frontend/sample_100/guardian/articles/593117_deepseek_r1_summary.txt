Guardian readers have shared a humorous collection of their pets dressed for Halloween via the newspaper's GuardianWitness platform. The photos showcase a wide array of costumed animals, often accompanied by witty owner captions detailing their pets' reactions. Highlights include Pearl the border terrier enduring her outfit for a sausage reward, Mylo the dog transformed into a handmade Ewok, and Blossom the guinea pig posing inside a pumpkin. Other entries feature pets like Poppy the cat ("At weekends I like to be called Satan"), Daisy the basset hound as a devil, and Zoe the staffordshire bull terrier in medieval attire. The collection reveals varying levels of pet compliance, from Murphy's resigned "sore head" to Sybil the cat sporting an orange-and-bats ensemble. Notably, The Venerable Mr Piddles was described as hating Halloween costumes despite his clerical outfit, while Alvaro the cat played a "cute little cat in the basket" trick. The photos capture the festive spirit and often reluctant participation of household pets during the spooky season.
