Football clubs across England commemorated the 25th anniversary of the Hillsborough disaster with widespread tributes during weekend matches. At Wembley Stadium, 96 seats were draped in Liverpool scarves and left vacant for the FA Cup semi-final between Arsenal and Wigan Athletic, symbolizing each life lost. Players, officials, and fans observed a minute’s silence before kick-off, with the names of victims displayed on stadium screens including Stoke’s Britannia Stadium. Commemorative wreaths were laid by both clubs and FA Chairman Greg Dyke at Wembley, while managers paid respects at other grounds. At Hillsborough Stadium, flowers adorned the memorial outside Sheffield Wednesday’s ground. Inside, 96 blue seats in the Leppings Lane End—where the 1989 crush occurred—were replaced with white seats bearing roses and inscribed cards honoring individual victims. Matches throughout England’s top eight divisions delayed kick-offs by seven minutes. Players wore black armbands, including Everton’s Tim Howard. Tributes extended to Sunderland’s pitch-center floral display, Newcastle fan messages, and banners at Fulham’s Craven Cottage. Minute’s silences were held at venues including Selhurst Park, St Mary’s (Southampton vs. Cardiff), and Newport County. The Hillsborough disaster on April 15, 1989, claimed 96 Liverpool fans during an FA Cup semi-final against Nottingham Forest due to overcrowding in the Leppings Lane terrace.
