The International Energy Agency warns global oil markets face worsening oversupply through 2016, potentially driving prices lower. Iran's return to the export market following lifted sanctions threatens to add 285 million barrels to stocks this year, countering production cuts elsewhere. Supply is projected to exceed demand by 1 million barrels per day for a third consecutive year. Weakening demand growth, driven primarily by China's economic slowdown, saw consumption plummet from 2.1 million barrels per day growth in Q3 2015 to just 1 million barrels per day in Q4. Simultaneously, Iran's re-entry could offset a forecasted 600,000 barrel-per-day decline from non-OPEC producers by mid-2016. Brent crude recently hit a 13-year low below $28 a barrel. The IEA stated prices "could go lower," emphasizing markets risk "drowning in oversupply." Storage capacity is becoming scarce, potentially forcing excess crude onto tankers at sea. While falling oil prices were previously seen as economically beneficial, concerns now focus on weakening global demand contributing to the slump. The agency noted significant uncertainty remains around Iran's ability to quickly place large volumes into an already saturated market.
