A temporary natural swimming pool functioning as a public art installation has opened in London's King's Cross redevelopment zone. The King’s Cross Pond Club utilizes an ecological filtration system where wetland plants and submerged vegetation naturally purify the water in a chemical-free closed loop. Juxtaposed against ongoing construction, the pond is encircled by pioneer plants, wildflowers, grasses and bushes designed to evolve seasonally. The installation will remain accessible for two years, with visitors required to book timed swimming sessions. Facilities include showers adjacent to the pond. Created by artists Marjetica Potrč and Ooze, the project merges urban development with environmental art, offering a controlled natural oasis within the cityscape.
