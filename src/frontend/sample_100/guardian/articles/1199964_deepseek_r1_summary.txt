Across Greece, haunting images reveal the frozen-in-time remnants of abandoned factories, bearing silent witness to decades of industrial decline. Photographs document deserted facilities from Elefsina to Xanthi and Larissa to Thessaloniki, scattered with artifacts left behind when operations ceased. Key sites include: * A cooking oil factory in Elefsina (closed 1996), its gate draped with a Greek flag and entrance featuring an old punching-in clock. * Multiple factories near Larissa: a marble processing plant (closed 2006) with a broken "Accountant’s Office" sign and discarded accounting books; a cotton spinning mill (closed 2000) displaying calendars from 2000 and boxes of thread; and a textile factory (closed 1995) with graffiti-covered, dust-coated windows and a rusty gate. * A nut factory near Xanthi (closed 1995) with abandoned documents. * Deserted grain silos standing before Mount Olympus near Larissa. * The Izola home appliance factory in Thebes, founded in 1930 and shuttered in 1986. * A fruit-packing plant near Xanthi (closed late 1990s) covered in graffiti. * A Thessaloniki factory closed in the early 1990s and a logistics company (closed 2012) where a discarded Christmas tree remains. These images capture the abrupt halt of industry, preserving poignant relics like calendars frozen at the year 2000, operational equipment, and personal items, illustrating the lasting physical scars of Greece's shifting economic landscape across multiple regions and industrial sectors over nearly three decades.
