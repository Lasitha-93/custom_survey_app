Five Nationals fans were rescued from a sinking boat on the Potomac River early Friday after attending Washington's playoff loss to the Dodgers, authorities reported. The incident occurred around 2 a.m. near the Woodrow Wilson Bridge in Alexandria, Virginia, when their 25-foot motorboat struck an unknown object—possibly a buoy marker—and began taking on water. Two nearby fishermen heard a loud bang and discovered the distressed vessel. They rescued all five occupants and transported them to shore at the Old Dominion Boat Club on King Street before alerting the U.S. Coast Guard. The D.C. Harbor Patrol later located the unoccupied boat with significant damage and impounded it for investigation. No injuries were reported. While the fishermen confirmed the rescued group had attended Thursday night's Nationals-Dodgers game, which ended in a 4-3 Washington loss, they declined media interviews. The boat club representative stated the facility was closed during the incident and had no knowledge of the rescue. The Nationals' defeat had already been compounded by Metro service disruptions from SafeTrack repairs affecting post-game transportation.
