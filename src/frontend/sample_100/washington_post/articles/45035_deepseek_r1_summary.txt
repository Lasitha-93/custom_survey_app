R&B singer Chris Brown's assault charge was reduced from a felony to a misdemeanor Monday in Washington D.C. Superior Court. Brown and his bodyguard, Christopher Hollosy, are accused of striking a man during an altercation outside the W Hotel early Saturday morning during Howard University's homecoming weekend. Both were released and face a maximum penalty of 180 days in jail and a $1,000 fine if convicted. Brown’s next court appearance is scheduled for November 25. The incident occurred around 4:30 a.m. after a nightclub event. Conflicting accounts exist: police reports cite a dispute over photographs, while court documents indicate the alleged victim attempted to join Brown in a photo after Brown posed with two women. One woman stated Brown made a derogatory remark before allegedly punching the man, with Hollosy also striking him. Brown denied hitting anyone, telling police his bodyguard "handled" the situation when someone tried boarding his tour bus. The victim suffered a broken nose. Brown’s attorney, Danny Onorato, asserted his client "committed no crime" and that Hollosy acted lawfully in protection. Both men appeared shackled in court Monday evening before being released. As a condition of release, Brown must report to his probation officer in California within 48 hours—a requirement stemming from his 2009 felony conviction for assaulting then-girlfriend Rihanna. That prior probation has been complicated by subsequent law enforcement encounters and questions about completed community service. The hearing drew significant media attention and public spectators, with supporters holding "Free Chris Brown" signs. Critics expressed frustration over courtroom crowding delaying other cases. Brown exited the courthouse amid cheers from fans gathered outside.
