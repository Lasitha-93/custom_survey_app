Alexandria Mayor Bill Euille lost his bid for a fifth term in Tuesday's Democratic primary, falling to Vice Mayor Allison Silberberg by 312 votes in a race dominated by concerns about development overwhelming the city's historic character. The defeat marks a significant shift after Euille's 12-year tenure as the city's first African American mayor. Silberberg, who campaigned on restoring public trust and implementing "balanced" development that preserves neighborhood scale, secured 52% of the vote against Euille and former mayor Kerry Donley. She emphasized reducing closed-door meetings and cited widespread voter frustration: "example after example of people feeling they were not being heard." Euille hasn't ruled out a November write-in campaign. The primary reflected broader regional tensions. Across Northern Virginia, Democratic contests centered on development pressures, school crowding, and traffic. In Fairfax County, incumbent Mason District Supervisor Penelope Gross defeated challenger Jessica Swanson, who criticized school funding and developer ties. Dan Storck won the nomination for Mount Vernon District's open seat. Arlington County saw Katie Cristol and Christian Dorsey secure Democratic nominations for two County Board seats, setting up a November battle over fiscal priorities. Silberberg, who faces no Republican opponent, had opposed Euille's waterfront redevelopment plan and returned developer donations during her campaign. Voters expressed strong desire for change, with one noting, "Donley and Euille? Been there, done that." Euille highlighted achievements including waterfront planning and attracting the National Science Foundation.
