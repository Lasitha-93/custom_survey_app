NASA's Solar Dynamics Observatory captured a massive coronal hole near the sun's south pole on New Year's Day. These dark regions form where magnetic fields extend into space instead of looping back to the solar surface, allowing particles to escape and reducing visible light. Unlike solar flares, coronal holes can persist for years—polar ones often exceeding five years—while changing shape. As they rotate past Earth, they emit high-speed particle streams that influence space weather. First photographed in the early 1970s by Skylab astronauts, these phenomena remain key subjects for solar research.
