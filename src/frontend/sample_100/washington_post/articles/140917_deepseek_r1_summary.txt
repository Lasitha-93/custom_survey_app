TV Land has removed "The Dukes of Hazzard" from its lineup amid controversy over the Confederate flag displayed on the show's iconic General Lee car. The decision follows Warner Bros.' halt on merchandise licensing and nationwide debates about Confederate symbols after the Charleston church shooting. Actor John Schneider, who played Bo Duke, condemned the move as misguided. He argued the show never promoted racism, stating: "The Dukes of Hazzard was and is no more a show seated in racism than Breaking Bad was a show seated in reality." Schneider tweeted a photo of alleged shooter Dylann Roof wearing a Gold's Gym shirt, sarcastically asking if its logo should now be considered racist. In a follow-up YouTube video titled "Can't we all watch TV?", Schneider called the cancellation "silly" and "an error," insisting the beloved series is being unfairly maligned. While acknowledging the Charleston tragedy should be the primary focus, he maintained: "I know 'The Dukes of Hazzard'. I love 'The Dukes of Hazzard'." Former cast member Ben Jones ("Cooter") echoed the defense on Facebook, writing in all caps that the flag represents Southern independence and family values, not racism. He accused critics of "cultural cleansing" and declared: "WE DESPISE RACISM." Their stance contrasts sharply with the NAACP's longstanding position. Los Angeles chapter president Geraldine Washington previously called the Confederate flag "a symbol of hatred" that creates division. The controversy revisits creators' past claims that the flag was innocently included as a cultural reference without political intent. Warner Bros. and TV Land haven't publicly detailed their reasons for halting merchandise and broadcasts. *Correction: Ben Jones served as a Democratic congressman from Georgia, not Republican.*
