**CMA Awards 2015: Stapleton Dominates, Timberlake Duet Steals Show** Chris Stapleton emerged as the breakout star at the 2015 CMA Awards, winning three trophies including Male Vocalist of the Year and Album of the Year for "Traveller." His electrifying duet with Justin Timberlake—melding "Tennessee Whiskey" and Timberlake’s "Drink You Away"—earned a standing ovation and propelled his album to No. 1 on iTunes. Little Big Town claimed Vocal Group of the Year and swept Song and Single of the Year for "Girl Crush." Florida Georgia Line secured Vocal Duo honors, while Miranda Lambert extended her Female Vocalist win streak to six years. Luke Bryan took Entertainer of the Year. **Top Moments**: Stapleton-Timberlake’s collaboration headlined a night of strong performances. Kacey Musgraves dazzled with "Dime Store Cowgirl," and Lambert delivered a resilient "Bathroom Sink" amid personal turmoil following ex-husband Blake Shelton’s pre-show confirmation of his relationship with Gwen Stefani. Sam Hunt showcased confidence with "Take Your Time." **Low Points**: Shelton and Lambert’s post-division awkwardness overshadowed segments, including Lambert’s emotional acceptance speech. Hosts Brad Paisley and Carrie Underwood’s monologue—featuring tepid jokes about Ashley Madison and political figures—fell flat. Thomas Rhett and Fall Out Boy’s disjointed collaboration and William Shatner’s unfunny "Star Wars" cameo also disappointed. *Key Winners*: - New Artist: Chris Stapleton - Music Video: Maddie & Tae ("Girl in a Country Song") - Musical Event: Keith Urban feat. Eric Church ("Raise ‘Em Up")
