Washington-area TV viewers overwhelmingly favored the NFL's Washington Redskins over MLB's Washington Nationals during simultaneous Sunday broadcasts, according to preliminary Nielsen ratings. The Redskins-Ravens game on Fox averaged approximately 740,000 local viewers—peaking above 1 million—while the Nationals-Dodgers playoff game on FS1 averaged about 135,000, peaking near 200,000. The disparity occurred despite both games being critical: the Nationals were in the NL Division Series, and the Redskins were in a key regular-season matchup. Observers noted fans at Nationals Park watching Redskins updates on mobile devices. Caveats include the Nationals' game being hastily rescheduled due to rain and airing on cable (versus the Redskins' network broadcast), potentially limiting visibility. The ratings challenge recent narratives—including a New York Times analysis—suggesting Washington is transitioning from a football-dominated market to a "baseball town." While acknowledging shifting fan dynamics, the data underscores football's persistent primacy locally. This pattern aligns with broader national viewership trends favoring NFL broadcasts over MLB postseason games.
