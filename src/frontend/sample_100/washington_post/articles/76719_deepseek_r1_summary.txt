Givenchy designer Riccardo Tisci staged a contemplative Spring 2016 runway show on September 11 in Lower Manhattan, using fashion to address themes of unity and remembrance near the 9/11 Memorial. Held at Pier 26 overlooking the Hudson River, the sunset presentation—art-directed by Marina Abramovic—featured performances symbolizing human connection across cultures, including spiritual chants representing six religions. The show, opened to the public via lottery, emphasized "forgiveness, inclusivity, new life, hope, and love" through diverse casting and Abramovic’s installations like actors in slow motion urging mindfulness. Tisci’s collection riffed on classic tuxedos with feminine lace accents and showcased intricate evening gowns with pearl beading and feathered trains. The event contrasted with typical fashion-week commercialism, positioning clothing within broader social dialogue. Similarly, designer Kerby Jean-Raymond of Pyer Moss confronted police violence and racial injustice in his show, incorporating graphic videos of victims and boots inscribed with "I can’t breathe." Both collections sparked debate about fashion’s capacity—and risks—in tackling societal issues, challenging perceptions of the industry as superficial amid tensions between artistic commentary and commercial demands.
