Russia has informed the United States it will not renew the Nunn-Lugar Cooperative Threat Reduction Program when it expires May 31. Moscow stated it no longer requires foreign aid and cited concerns about potential leaks of nuclear security information. The 21-year-old program, costing roughly $500 million annually, secured Soviet-era weapons after the bloc's collapse. Key achievements included removing nuclear arms from Ukraine, Kazakhstan, and Belarus; deactivating over 7,600 nuclear warheads; destroying 902 intercontinental ballistic missiles and 33 submarines; and securing 24 nuclear weapons storage sites. Russia's Foreign Ministry emphasized it isn't abandoning weapons security efforts but seeks "a new, more modern legal framework" for cooperation. This follows Russia's recent expulsion of USAID and UNICEF's planned departure. The decision occurs amid heightened anti-American rhetoric from the Kremlin and coincides with the primary defeat of Senator Richard Lugar, a key architect of the program. His co-sponsor, former Senator Sam Nunn, expressed hope that changes would strengthen the partnership. Russia maintains the aid program closures are unrelated to ending Nunn-Lugar.
